Migration Corridor
The wildebeests follow an anti-clockwise route starting off in the Ndutu plains in southern Serengeti in Jan–Mar, usually a calving season. They then move westwards and north through the Western Corridor in Apr–Jun, crossing into Maasai Mara in Jul–Sep and then returning south in Oct–Dec.

Named Collared Wildebeests

GPS fixes data from 4 named collared wildebeests were analysed for seasonal displacement patterns. Displacement was calculated relative to where they were in January, the Ndutu Plains in southern Serengeti, providing a baseline for tracking migration across the year. In addition, the data had collared zebras and were included to provide broader context on multi-species migration dynamics. Together, these data illustrate the coordinated, seasonal movement of large herbivores across the Serengeti–Mara ecosystem.
